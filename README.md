
# Metal Magsafe Wallet
A metal magsafe wallet designed to be rugged but comfortable. High tolerances and tight fits, designed to fit 2-3 cards comfortably and keep them inside. I built this project because I was tired of my plastic magsafe wallets breaking. I enjoyed their egonomic feel and slim form factor but did not like the fragility of each one. After 2-3 months every single one would break and crack down the side. I setout to build a metal wallet that could be easily taken appart and fixed. Oh no you sheered the screws off, well take them out and put new ones in problem solved. This project was designed on Fusion 360, and here was the process. I started with a base design of my wallet that I curently have (Speck Quicklock). I designed a base outline leaving ~2-4mm on each side and ~2mm on the top to allow of material. Then I designed the cutout, which had to have enough space for three cards and each card being 54x85.6x.7 mm and clearances because I did not want to use friction. Then I realized that there would be no way to construct a one part design without an internal lever to apply pressure so I cut ther design in half, added 2M screw holes, and built out the rest of my design. I researched many types of springs and spring mecanisms, I think the most viable actual spring would be a Torison bar, but I could not find one that would fit. I recognised that just a piece of metal ribbion when loaded also applies pressure like a spring and decided to use that. After calculating the nececary size, thickness, and length. I made further screw holes in the top to allow for this addition. I also added a magsafe cutout to allow for a 1mm magsafe addition for magsafe functionality. Then I chose proper products and had my part quoted.
